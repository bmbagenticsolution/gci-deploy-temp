## Section A. IC Charter Elements

**A1. Purpose**

The Investment Committee (IC) of [FAMILY OFFICE NAME] is the body that approves material investment decisions on behalf of the family. The IC operates under the family charter dated [DATE] and the Investment Policy Statement dated [DATE].

**A2. Composition**

- Principal or principal's representative (chair, voting): [NAME]
- Chief Investment Officer (voting): [NAME]
- Independent investment professional 1 (voting): [NAME], term ends [DATE]
- Independent investment professional 2 (voting): [NAME], term ends [DATE]
- Next-generation family member (observer / voting): [NAME], status [OBSERVER OR VOTING]
- External asset class advisor (non-voting, by invitation): as required

**A3. Voting and quorum**

- Quorum: [N] voting members present, including at least one independent
- Standard resolution: simple majority of voting members present
- Reserved matter (charter amendment, IPS amendment, principal change): [SUPERMAJORITY OR UNANIMITY]
- Tie-breaker: chair has casting vote on standard resolutions only

**A4. Cadence**

- Quarterly regular meetings: [MONTHS]
- Annual deep performance review: [MONTH]
- Annual strategy day: [MONTH]
- Ad-hoc meetings: convened with [N] business days notice for material decisions

**A5. Term lengths**

- Independent members: 3 years renewable, maximum 2 consecutive terms
- Family members: per family charter
- Rotation: stagger independent terms so at most one rotates per year

## Section C. Investment Paper Standard

Every proposal requiring IC approval is written up in this standard format and distributed at least 7 days before the meeting.

1. **Executive summary with the recommendation** (1 page)
2. **Investment thesis** (2 to 3 pages)
3. **Manager or counterparty diligence** (2 to 3 pages)
4. **Fee and structure analysis** (1 to 2 pages)
5. **Risk factors with mitigants** (1 to 2 pages)
6. **Fit with Investment Policy Statement and current portfolio** (1 page)
7. **Liquidity and exit profile** (1 page)
8. **Recommendation with proposed size, terms, and conditions** (1 page)

Total length: 8 to 15 pages plus appendices.

## Section E. Pre-Meeting Checklist (CIO and Office)

- [ ] Agenda finalised 10 days before meeting
- [ ] Performance pack drafted: portfolio vs IPS benchmarks
- [ ] Manager updates collected from external managers
- [ ] Investment papers drafted for above-threshold proposals
- [ ] Conflicts of interest pre-circulation to flag potential recusals
- [ ] Pipeline status updated
- [ ] Regulatory and tax bulletin appended if relevant
- [ ] Distribution and liquidity request from principal collected
- [ ] Pack distributed 7 days before meeting

## Section F. In-Meeting Protocol

- [ ] Chair declares meeting open and confirms quorum
- [ ] Conflicts declared by each member
- [ ] Recusals taken and recorded for affected items
- [ ] Previous minutes approved
- [ ] Action items reviewed
- [ ] Agenda followed in order; chair manages time
- [ ] Each item: CIO presents, IC challenges, decision recorded
- [ ] Resolutions captured at time of decision
- [ ] Action items captured with owner and deadline
- [ ] Date of next meeting confirmed

## Section G. Post-Meeting Protocol

- [ ] Draft minutes circulated within 7 days
- [ ] Action items entered into family office tracker
- [ ] Investment decisions communicated to relevant external counterparties
- [ ] Material decisions logged in family office decision register
- [ ] Manager terminations or appointments processed by COO
- [ ] Minutes approved at next meeting

## Section H. Conflicts of Interest Categories Requiring Recusal

Recusal required for:

1. Related-party transactions (family member or family office staff has a financial interest in the counterparty)
2. Family member commercial interest in the manager being voted on
3. Personal trading overlap with a proposed listed position
4. Fee-receiving relationship with the proposed manager
5. Board, advisory, or shareholder position in the target investment

Borderline cases escalated to chair and resolved before relevant agenda item.

---

## Section I. Minutes Standard

Minutes record decisions, not deliberations. Each set of minutes includes:

- Date, time, location, attendees, members absent
- Conflicts declared and recusals taken
- Resolutions passed (decision, vote, conditions)
- Action items with owner and deadline
- Material discussion points (without verbatim transcript)
- Date of next meeting
- Approval of previous minutes
- Signed by chair upon approval

Retention: permanent in family office record system.

## Section J. Annual Self-Review (12 questions)

1. Are agenda items getting appropriate time and depth?
2. Are paper standards being met by the CIO and external advisors?
3. Are members preparing adequately before meetings?
4. Is the threshold matrix calibrated correctly?
5. Are conflicts being declared and managed appropriately?
6. Are minutes capturing decisions accurately?
7. Are action items being closed within agreed timelines?
8. Are external managers being reviewed appropriately?
9. Is IC composition right (skills, geography, family representation)?
10. Are independent members effective?
11. Is the family voice appropriately represented?
12. What should change for the next year?

- Disclosures to family members outside the IC governed by family charter
- Disclosures to external parties require chair approval
- Records held in [PRIMARY SYSTEM] with [BACKUP SYSTEM]

## Adoption Notes

- Adopt the charter at a constitutional family meeting with all principals present.
- Sign and date the charter; circulate signed version to all members.
- Schedule the first quarterly meeting within 30 days of adoption.
- Plan first annual self-review 12 months after adoption.
- Review the charter every 3 years or on triggering events (principal change, family expansion, material asset change).
