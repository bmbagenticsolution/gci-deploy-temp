What each persona most needs to know before investing

First-time GCC investor - needs regulatory orientation first. Foreign ownership rules by sector, mainland vs free zone vs DIFC structures, visa and residency options, and a clear picture of what a market entry process actually involves. The conviction question is: is this the right market for someone entering from outside?

Family Office / HNWI - cares about capital security above growth. Asset-backed structures, yield profile, repatriation clarity, and counterparty quality. The conviction question is: can this capital be preserved and compounded safely in this market?

Institutional PE / VC - needs investable market size, management team quality indicators, exit routes (trade sale, secondary, IPO on DFM or ADX), and clear valuation methodology. The conviction question is: can a fund-sized position be built and exited within the mandate window?

Strategic Operator - needs to understand if their business model translates culturally and commercially. Consumer behavior differences, competitive response from incumbents, regulatory requirements for their specific sector, and realistic customer acquisition costs. The conviction question is: will this business work here, and can it be operated profitably?

GCC Intra-regional investor - already understands the macro but needs the vertical or emirate specific intelligence they are missing. Sector depth, competitive saturation, regulatory nuances for a new vertical, or the specific dynamics of an emirate outside their home market. The conviction question is: does this specific opportunity represent better risk-adjusted return than what I already hold?
